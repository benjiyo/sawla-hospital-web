Background Overview

Background & History

Sawla General Hospital is the only general hospital in Gofa Zone. It was established in 2000 EC and is located about 516 km from Addis Ababa. The hospital serves a catchment population of more than 1,500,000 people and plays a crucial role in providing essential, quality healthcare services to the surrounding communities.

The facility employs a diverse workforce dedicated to high-quality care: 269 health professionals and 163 non-clinical staff. Health professionals include: 8 specialists, 22 general practitioners, 81 nurses, 27 midwives, 21 health officers, 29 clinical pharmacists and druggists, 18 laboratory professionals, 3 radiology technologists, 2 environmental health professionals, plus other supportive staff. Our specialists include gynaecologists, internists, paediatricians, ophthalmologists, surgeons, orthopaedician and pathologists.

  • Catchment population: 1,500,000+
  • Annual outpatient visits: 173,000
  • Annual inpatient admissions: 6,240
  • Annual emergency visits: 6,180
  • Annual deliveries: 2,760 (including 993 C-sections)
  • Annual surgeries (elective & emergency): 2,460
  • ICU admissions (annual): 350
  • NICU admissions (annual): 820
